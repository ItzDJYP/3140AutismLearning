Understanding Autism

General Overview

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a developmental condition that affects how people communicate, interact, and experience the world. Every person with autism is unique, and their abilities and challenges vary.

What is Autism Spectrum Disorder?

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a complex neurodevelopmental condition characterized by differences in social communication, behavior, and sensory processing. People with autism may experience the world in unique ways, and their strengths and challenges can vary widely.

Types and Traits of Autism

Autism presents differently in each person. Some common traits include:

  • Challenges with social communication and interaction
  • Repetitive behaviors or routines
  • Sensory sensitivities (to sounds, lights, textures, etc.)
  • Strong interests in specific topics
  • Unique ways of learning and problem-solving

How to Support Individuals with Autism

  • Be patient and understanding.
  • Use clear, simple language.
  • Encourage routine and structure.
  • Allow extra processing time.
  • Celebrate their strengths and interests.
  • Provide sensory-friendly environments when possible.
  • Respect their individuality and preferences.

Levels of Autism

ASD is categorized into three levels based on the amount of support an individual needs. These levels help professionals tailor interventions and support strategies:

  1. Level 1: Requiring Support
    • Difficulty initiating social interactions; misses social cues.
    • Organization/planning challenges that impact independence.
    • Communication differences noticeable but less severe.
  2. Level 2: Requiring Substantial Support
    • Marked differences in verbal and nonverbal communication.
    • Interactions may be limited to narrow interests or topics.
    • More apparent repetitive behaviors and difficulty with change.
  3. Level 3: Requiring Very Substantial Support
    • Severe differences in social communication and interaction.
    • Very limited ability to engage or respond to others.
    • Extreme difficulty with change; highly restrictive behaviors.
